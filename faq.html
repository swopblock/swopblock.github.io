FAQ

Is Swopblock a blockchain?

Yes and No. Swopblock is a virtual blockchain that is an abstraction of preexisting blockchains (i.e. BTC and ETH), therefore Swopblock does not need its own separate database file for trade information. Swopblock’s trade information is embedded within the preexisting blockchains. This enables trades to leverage the proven securities within these preexisting blockchains.

Is Swopblock a cryptocurrency?

Swopblock is a protocol that employs SWBL to operate the Swopblock protocol.

What is SWBL? (pronounced ‘swobble’)

SWBL is the trading symbol for value within the Swopblock protocol. For example, Swopblock has a total issue capital base of 51,200,000 SWBL.

What is the Swopblock Network?

The Swopblock Network is the collection of cryptocurrency blockchain networks that have been adapted to trade SWBL, enabling the Swopblock protocol.

How are Swopblock cross-chain trades autonomous?

Swopblock is a decentralized collection of autonomous bots that collectively execute trades bot-to-bot in response to user trade orders. These autonomous bots build order transactions and execution transactions and broadcast them to the native blockchain networks to fulfill the user’s order. For example, in a user order to exchange BTC for ETH the autonomous bots will build native blockchain order transactions, broadcast these transactions in both blockchain networks and fulfill the order with execution transactions that are broadcast to these networks to complete the order.

This enables peers to trade without the need to identify a counterparty and eliminates the need of an exchange party. In otherwords Swopblock is a peer-to-bot-to-bot-to-peer exchange.

What big problem does Swopblock solve? Don’t decentralized exchanges already exist?

Decentralized crypto exchanges currently only offer secure-realtime trades that are limited to a single crypto market within a specific blockchain platform. Swopblock offers secure-realtime trades on a specific blockchain platform and extends BEYOND a single blockchain offering universal cross-blockchain trades.

What makes Swopblock Network unique in the Crypto Market?

The crypto market has not yet sufficiently solved the exchange problem. Centralized exchanges have lost or stolen billions worth of cryptos in the last few years. Decentralized exchanges only offer ‘on platform’ exchanges and are not able to perform cross-network/cross-blockchain trading. Of course peers can meet up or use Atomic Swaps, but these exchanges are slow and unresponsive to the continually fluctuating crypto market. The Swopblock exchange protocol solves all three of these problems - offers security, universal access, and happens in realtime.

Does Swopblock use Atomic Swaps?

No. Swopblock does NOT use Atomic Swap technology. Swopblock trades happen in realtime taking into account market fluctuations. This way neither trader can take advantage of time delays and use price fluctuations to their advantage.

There are several problems with Atomic Swaps. Although Atomic Swaps offer a decentralized approach to exchange, these swaps rely on Hash Time-Locked Contracts, or HTLCs. This technology can be slow. Bitcoin swaps can take over an hour to finalize. As we all know, crypto prices can fluctuation quickly, this can incentivize one party to either wait until the market price fluctuates in favor of their trade, or they simply cancel the order to ensure the trade doesn’t disfavor their own potential value. Another problem with Atomic Swaps is that not every crypto is compatible. To be compatible cryptos must share a hashing algorithm and must be programmable.

Swopblock offers market order capabilities (patent pending) that allow peers to buy and sell into and out of a crypto market without the need to identify the counterparty. Traders never lose control of their value. Trades are autonomous and react in realtime to continuous crypto market fluctuations. As soon as an order is made by one party the Swopblock protocol autonomously finds a counterparty and executes the trade.

Does Swopblock use miners or is the SWBL pre-mined?

No. All SWBL is created from the start (a 51,200,000 SWBL initial coin offering, ICO). This initial supply is created as an abstraction on an existing blockchain (such as BTC) in order to launch trading on that particular blockchain. And, when new blockchains are brought onto the Swopblock Network, that new blockchain is issued its own supply of 51,200,000 SWBL. Swopblock essentially duplicates itself without diluting the value of the ICO supply of 51,200,000 SWBL. This process continues and propagates the Swopblock Network as more and more cryptos are brought onto it. This allows blockchain developers to avoid centralized exchange listing fees (sometimes in the 10s of millions) and simply customize the Swopblock plugin to bring their blockchain onto the Swopblock Network.

How is the Swopblock Network incentivized?

Swopblock offers a maximum reward of 1.5625% of each transaction amount as a reward to the party that completes the autonomous trade transaction. This reward covers the native blockchain fees resulting in fee-free trade. Users will (directly or through policy) voluntarily offer native blockchain transaction fees to speed confirmation of orders.

How is it possible ANY cryptocurrency blockchain can join the Swopblock Network?

Swopblock is an open source exchange protocol that provides developers an API for the customization of implementations necessary to join the Swopblock Network. SWBL essentially acts as a blockchain adaptor to the Swopblock protocol.
